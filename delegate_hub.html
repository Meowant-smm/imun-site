Preparation

Statement of Position (SoP)

A Statement of Position is a formal written document submitted before committee sessions. It outlines your country's official stance on the agenda topic, relevant national policies, and proposed solutions.

Structure

  • Country name and committee
  • Country's position on the topic
  • Past actions taken by the country
  • Proposed solutions and calls to action

Example

SoP — UNSC — Climate Security
Federal Republic of Germany

The Federal Republic of Germany firmly believes that climate change poses an existential threat to global peace and security. Germany has committed to achieving carbon neutrality by 2045 and calls upon all member states to adopt binding emissions reduction targets. Germany proposes the establishment of a UN Climate Security Task Force to monitor climate-induced conflict zones and coordinate humanitarian response.

Speaking

General Speakers List (GSL)

The General Speakers List is the primary speaking order in committee. Delegates deliver a formal speech (typically 60–90 seconds) outlining their country's position on the agenda topic.

Tips

  • State your country's position clearly in the first 15 seconds
  • Reference real national policies or past UN resolutions
  • End with a call to action or proposal

Example

GSL Speech — UNHRC — Freedom of Press
Kingdom of Norway

Honourable Chair, distinguished delegates — The Kingdom of Norway stands unwavering in its commitment to press freedom as a fundamental human right. Norway ranks consistently among the top nations in the World Press Freedom Index and urges this committee to adopt a binding resolution mandating independent media protection mechanisms in all member states. Norway calls upon delegates to move beyond rhetoric and commit to enforceable international standards. Thank you.

Caucus

Unmoderated Caucus

An Unmoderated Caucus is an informal recess from formal committee proceedings. Delegates move freely around the room to negotiate, build blocs, draft working papers, and form coalitions.

How to Motion

Motion for an Unmoderated Caucus of [X] minutes for the purpose of [bloc building / working paper drafting / informal debate].

Tips

  • Use this time to find allies with similar positions
  • Begin drafting working papers or resolutions
  • Approach opposing blocs to find compromise language

Example Motion

Motion for Unmoderated Caucus

The delegate of Brazil moves for an Unmoderated Caucus of 15 minutes for the purpose of working paper drafting.

Caucus

Moderated Caucus

A Moderated Caucus is a structured, topic-specific debate within the committee. The Chair calls on delegates individually. Speaking times are shorter than GSL — typically 30–45 seconds.

How to Motion

Motion for a Moderated Caucus of [total time] with [speaking time] per delegate on the topic of [specific subtopic].

Tips

  • Pick a narrow, focused subtopic
  • Use it to steer debate toward your bloc's agenda
  • Keep speeches punchy and directive

Example Motion

Motion for Moderated Caucus

The delegate of India moves for a Moderated Caucus of 10 minutes with 45 seconds per delegate on the topic of funding mechanisms for climate adaptation in developing nations.

Documentation

Resolution & Operative Items

A Resolution is the final formal document passed by the committee. It contains Preambulatory Clauses (context and background) and Operative Clauses (binding or recommending actions).

Preambulatory Clauses

Set the context. Begin with words like: Recalling, Recognizing, Affirming, Deeply concerned, Noting with appreciation

Recalling United Nations Security Council Resolution 1325 on Women, Peace and Security,

Operative Clauses

State the actions. Begin with words like: Urges, Calls upon, Encourages, Decides, Requests, Condemns

Urges all member states to allocate a minimum of 2% of their national defence budgets toward conflict prevention and peacebuilding initiatives;

Full Example — UNGA — Cybersecurity

Operative Clause

Calls upon all member states to establish national cybersecurity agencies with clear mandates to protect critical infrastructure, and further requests the Secretary-General to submit a report on best practices in cyber governance by the next General Assembly session;

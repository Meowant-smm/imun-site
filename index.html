International Press (IP)

The International Press committee simulates the role of global media during a Model UN conference. Delegates act as journalists, photographers, and editors — documenting proceedings across all committees.

IP delegates interview other delegates, write articles, produce newsletters, and may even create short broadcasts. This committee is ideal for delegates who excel in writing, critical analysis, and storytelling under pressure.

United Nations Security Council (UNSC)

The UNSC is one of the most prestigious and fast-paced committees in any MUN conference. Composed of 15 member states — including 5 permanent members with veto power — the Security Council is responsible for maintaining international peace and security.

Delegates will address active conflicts, peacekeeping mandates, sanctions, and crisis simulations. This committee demands sharp negotiation skills, rapid decision-making, and a deep understanding of geopolitics.

United Nations General Assembly (UNGA)

The General Assembly is the chief deliberative and representative organ of the United Nations. All 193 member states have equal representation, making it the most inclusive forum for multilateral discussion.

Delegates will debate a wide range of global issues — from sustainable development and disarmament to international law and human rights. UNGA resolutions, while non-binding, carry enormous moral authority and shape global norms.

United Nations Human Rights Council (UNHRC)

The UNHRC is responsible for the promotion and protection of all human rights around the globe. It addresses situations of human rights violations and makes recommendations on them.

Delegates will engage with pressing humanitarian issues — freedom of expression, rights of refugees, gender equality, and the protection of minorities. This committee calls for empathy, legal acumen, and a commitment to justice.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the date and venue? +
The conference runs primarily in April 2027. The venue will be shared exclusively with registered delegates ahead of the event.
Do I need prior MUN experience? +
No. We welcome both first-time delegates seeking to learn and seasoned debaters looking for a prestigious platform.
How do committee allocations work? +
Allocations are processed on a rolling basis. Upon registration and payment, you will receive your portfolio via email.
What is the dress code? +
Western formals or traditional national attire. As a highly prestigious summit, a strict adherence to diplomatic dress is expected.
